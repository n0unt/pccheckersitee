Zevora only collects data that is directly relevant to detecting cheating. We do not sell data, run ads, or share results with anyone outside of the league that requested your scan.

1. Overview

Zevora is a forensic screenshare tool used by competitive Roblox flag football leagues to detect cheating. This privacy policy explains what data is collected when you run the scanner, how it is stored, and who has access to it.

By running the Zevora scanner, you consent to the data collection described in this policy. This consent is explicitly captured through the dual-checkbox agreement in the scanner before any data is collected.

2. What We Collect

When the scanner runs, it reads the following categories of data from your PC:

🎮 Roblox & Game Data

Roblox usernames, user IDs, log files, FastFlag configurations, place IDs from recent sessions, and Bloxstrap/launcher configuration data.

💬 Discord & Communication

Discord account usernames and IDs found in the app cache and browser storage. We do not read message content or DMs.

📂 Execution & File History

Names and paths of recently executed programs (from Prefetch, BAM, AppCompat, ShellBags, and similar Windows forensic artifacts). File hashes and metadata for flagged files. PowerShell and command history.

🖥️ System Information

Operating system version, install date, BIOS date, factory reset evidence, logon/logoff session history (past 15 days), and drive information.

💉 Memory & Process Data

Names and paths of DLLs loaded inside the Roblox process while running, used to detect injected cheat software. No personal files or documents are read.

🌐 Network Information

VPN indicators and network adapter names. Your IP address is collected server-side during submission but is not included in reports visible to agents.

3. How We Use It

Data collected by the scanner is used exclusively to:

  • Determine whether cheating software was present or used on the scanned PC
  • Generate a scan report for the league agent who requested the screenshare
  • Allow league administrators to review evidence and make disciplinary decisions

We do not use your data for advertising, analytics, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to cheat detection in the league context that requested your scan.

4. Who Sees Your Data

Scan results are accessible to:

  • League agents — the person who issued your PIN and their league administrators
  • Zevora admins and owners — for platform maintenance and dispute resolution only

Results are not shared with other leagues, third parties, or the public. IP addresses are redacted from all agent-visible reports.

5. Data Storage & Retention

Scan results are stored in a secure PostgreSQL database hosted on Railway. Access is restricted to authorized accounts only.

Scan data is retained for as long as it is relevant to league operations. League owners and admins may delete individual scan records at any time through the dashboard. If you believe your scan data should be removed, contact us using the information below.

6. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Know what data was collected about you — contact us and we will provide a copy of your scan report
  • Request deletion of your scan data from our servers
  • Withdraw consent — note that withdrawal does not undo a scan that has already been completed and submitted
  • Report unauthorized use of Zevora on your device
