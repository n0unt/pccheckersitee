2. What Zevora Does

Zevora is a forensic PC scanner designed to detect cheating in competitive Roblox football leagues. When run, it reads forensic artifacts from the local PC and transmits results to the agent who requested the scan. It does not install anything permanently and does not run in the background after the scan completes.

Zevora may read and transmit the following:

  • System and OS configuration, installation history, and factory reset evidence
  • Records of programs run, installed, opened, or deleted — including prefetch, registry, event logs, shellbags, recycle bin, and raw MFT records that may survive a factory reset
  • Roblox and Discord account identifiers and game log data
  • File system metadata including filenames, paths, and timestamps
  • DLLs and modules loaded in running processes (injection detection)
  • PowerShell and command history
  • Network adapter information and VPN indicators
  • FastFlag configuration data
  • Power on/off and logon/logoff session history

4. Data Collection & Storage

Scan results are stored on secure servers and are accessible only to the authorized agent who issued the PIN, their league administrators, and the Zevora platform owner. Results are used solely to determine whether cheating occurred during a league screenshare session.

IP addresses collected during scans are not stored in publicly viewable reports. Zevora does not share scan results with any third parties outside the requesting league.
